Why this matters more in health than anywhere else

Health is one of the highest-intent things people now ask AI assistants about: "best telehealth for X," "is Y covered," "alternatives to Z." When an AI engine can't read a provider's site, it falls back to whoever it can read: a competitor, a directory, or a generic source. The gaps among the D-graders were consistent and entirely fixable:

  • JavaScript-only homepages. A client-rendered SPA serves AI crawlers that don't execute JS a near-empty page.
  • No JSON-LD structured data (no MedicalOrganization, Organization, or FAQPage), so engines have nothing machine-readable to quote.
  • No discoverable sitemap on the lowest scorers.

None of this requires touching the product or making medical claims. It's web hygiene that makes an existing site legible to AI search. The A-graders (Headspace, Teladoc) simply ship server-readable content, structured data, a clean robots.txt, and a sitemap.
