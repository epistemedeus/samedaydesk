The irony, and the lesson

Perplexity, an AI answer engine, scored a 60 (C). LlamaIndex, whose entire product is about making data readable by LLMs, scored a 53 (D), the lowest in the set. This isn't a knock on their products; it's a reflection of how their marketing sites are built. The pattern among the C's and D's was consistent:

  • JavaScript-only rendering. A client-rendered SPA serves crawlers that don't run JS a near-empty shell. The product is great; the homepage an AI crawler sees is thin.
  • Little or no JSON-LD structured data, so engines have no machine-readable description to quote.
  • No discoverable sitemap on the lowest scorers.

The A-graders (Cohere, Cursor, ElevenLabs, Pinecone, Suno, Lovable, Glean) simply ship server-readable content, structured data, a clean robots.txt, and a sitemap. None of it is exotic. It's hygiene. And if the companies building AI are leaving citations on the table, almost every other business is too.
