Infrastructure aces it; consumer brands lag

The split is striking. The companies whose customers are developers (Plaid, Brex, Mercury, Wise) ship clean, server-readable, structured sites and scored at the top. The lowest scorers were consumer-facing brands (Klarna, Chime, Ramp, Gusto) whose marketing sites lean on JavaScript-heavy rendering with little structured data, leaving AI engines little to read. The recurring gaps:

  • JavaScript-only homepages that serve AI crawlers a near-empty shell.
  • No JSON-LD structured data (Organization, FAQPage, FinancialProduct), so engines can't classify or quote the brand.
  • No discoverable sitemap on the lowest scorers.

None of this requires regulatory review or product changes: it's web hygiene that makes an existing site legible to AI search. Given how often people now ask AI assistants for financial-product recommendations, the consumer fintechs have the most to gain.
